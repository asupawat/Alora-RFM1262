Circuitrocks Alora RFM1262 Radio Transceiver Featherwing


Do you need to send data over distances that cannot be achieved with WiFi or Bluetooth, but GSM/LTE technology is not affordable or applicable?

You need to send data from your water quality sensors around your fish pond to a gateway? Or you want to collect values from your soil moisture sensors from your crop fields? That is what these modules are designed for.


Based on eBytes E22 module this Long Range Low Power module is using the latest Semtech LoRa® transceivers, the SX1262. This new generation of chips out-performs the existing radio chips like SX1272 or the RFM95/96 modules.


The Alora RFM1262 sub-GHz radio transceivers are ideal for long range wireless applications. The devices is designed for long battery life with just 4.2 mA of active receive current consumption. The Alora RFM1262can transmit up to +22 dBm with highly efficient integrated power amplifiers.


Applications:

The level of integration and the low consumption within SX1261/2 enable a new generation of Internet of Things applications.

  • Remote control applications

Specifications:

  • Continuous frequency coverage from 150 MHz to 960 MHz allows the support of all major sub-GHz ISM bands around the world.

  • Supports LoRa® modulation for LPWAN use cases and (G)FSK modulation for legacy use cases

  • The devices are designed to comply with the physical layer requirements of the LoRaWAN(TM) specification released by the LoRa Alliance(TM).

  • Measures 2.0”x0.9”x0.28” (51mmx23mmx8mm) without the headers soldered in

  • Light as a feather

  • ebyte E22-900M22S module with the Semtech SX1262 LoRa chip

  • SPI communication protocol

  • Simple assignment of interrupt and control signals for different micro controllers.

  • 4 mounting holes

  • u.FL connector or alternative solder hole for wire antenna and option to solder a SMA antenna connector
